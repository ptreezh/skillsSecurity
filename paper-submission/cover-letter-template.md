We are submitting our manuscript for consideration for publication in [Journal Name]. This paper addresses the governance challenges of AI agent skill ecosystems—a timely and critical topic as these ecosystems rapidly expand across industry platforms.

## Key Contributions

1. **Conceptual Innovation**: We propose and systematically define "responsibility vacuum in agent skill ecosystems," a novel concept that extends software supply chain governance and algorithmic accountability literature to the emerging domain of AI agent skills.

2. **Theoretical Advancement**: Drawing on the Pacing Problem theory (Marchant et al., 2011), Collingridge's Dilemma (1980), and Anticipatory Governance (Guston, 2014), we construct a three-layer analytical framework—lag diagnosis, risk characterisation, and anticipatory response—that explains why governance lags far behind technology deployment.

3. **Empirical Rigor**: Our cross-platform audit covers 2,600+ skills across five mainstream platforms (Zhipu Z.AI, Coze, WorkBuddy, Claude Skills, and OpenClaw/ClawHub), providing robust evidence that governance deficiency rates approach 100% across all platforms.

4. **Practical Implications**: We develop the "Responsibility Shield" prototype and R-Skills governance standard, demonstrating a technically feasible pathway for embedding responsibility tracing mechanisms into skill ecosystems.
