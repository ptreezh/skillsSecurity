## Abstract

This paper presents a systematic analysis of 2,600+ skills across five mainstream agent platforms through cross-platform auditing. The audit results reveal a consistent finding: governance deficiency rates across all platforms approach or reach 100% under Wieringa's (2020) four-dimensional algorithmic accountability framework. Drawing on the Pacing Problem theory (Marchant et al., 2011), Collingridge's Dilemma (1980), and Anticipatory Governance (Guston, 2014), this paper constructs a "lag diagnosis–risk characterisation–anticipatory response" three-layer analytical framework. The paper develops the "Responsibility Shield" conceptual prototype and R-Skills governance standard, exploring a technically feasible pathway for embedding responsibility tracing mechanisms before the governance lag window closes.
